Homelessness is a major issue in today's world. There exist many of us well off, however, the minority suffer and here are some reasons why this should be brought to light!

Facts You Should Know

Homelessness in the UK

  • 1) Councils in UK spend about 1.15 Billion GBP and 845 million GBP on services for the homeless and in providing accomodation temporarily.

  • 2) In Fall 2017 there were known to be 4751 homeless people across Great Britain, London represented 24% of the England total where 14% were women, 20% non UK nations and 8% under 25 years of age. However, this number is said to be underestimated with CHAIN. Around 8,108 people were said to have slept rough in London alone last year.

  • 3) About 300,00 people are classed as homeless in UK. Every one in 59 in London and one in 27 in Newham. 47% of those sleepers in London were said to struggle with mental health issues in the year 2013.

    • (Facts and figures courtesy: The Big Issue)

Reasons for Homelessness

Why are there such large numbers when it comes to homelessness?

  • 1) Poor physical or mental health problems, alcoholism and drug issues, bereavement, crimes are individual factors that make people more vulnerable to homelessness.

  • 2) Poverty, housing supply and affordability, unemployment, income policies can stand as structural factors that cause poverty.

  • 3) Both the above factors are interrelated. Individual factors can arise from structural factors and vice versa. Poverty or lack of education can cause individual factor issues while poor working skills, inability to work at one's best capacity due to personal issues (individual factors) may lead to employment being at risk. Hence the two are related and can unfortunately lead to being on the streets.

  • 4) Homelessness impacts individuals and communities in many ways. Since it brings in feelings of isolation, chances of taking drugs or experiencing major phsyical and mental health problems becomes a greater possibility.

  • 5) It is also known that the longer a person struggles in the position of being homeless, the harder it is to get back on their feet. Involvement in crimes and anti-social behavior becomes more likely.

  • 6) Homeless people are likely to sleep rough, be involved in street drinking, beg, indulge in prostitution.

    • (Facts and figures courtesy: Homeless Link)
